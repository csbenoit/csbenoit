Target disks

MAPS targeted five well-known protoplanetary disks to explore in depth chemically. The properties of these disks are listed below, and are also available in a convenient python dictionary via our data download page.

 

IM Lup

IM Lup is a Solar-mass star that hosts a young (~1 Myr), massive, and radially-extended disk. The IM Lup disk hosts the largest pebble disk (~260 au) in the DSHARP sample (Huang+18b) and a CO disk out to nearly 900 au (Cleeves+16c). Continuum images reveal the presence of spiral arms in mm grains, while scattered light observations show a highly flared, multi-ringed disk in small dust grains. Despite its youth, the IM Lup disk shows evidence of grain growth (Lommen+07). The outer portion of the CO gas disk appears fluffy or envelope-like, possibly indicating a photoevaporative wind (Haworth+17). IM Lup has been also been observed in several molecular lines (Oberg+11) and shows a striking double-ring structure in DCO+ 3-2, due to a change in gas-phase C and O abundance at the pebble disk edge (Oberg+15b). Modeling indicates that CO is underabundant by a factor of ~20 compared to molecular clouds due to missing gas-phase carbon and oxygen. No depletions were found in nitrogen, which results in an elevated gas-phase N/O ratio (Cleeves+18). Spitzer observations revealed the presence of weak OH and H2O emission in the inner disk, suggesting the IM Lup disk is in an intermediate stage of water depletion with some water vapor still present (Banzatti+17).
